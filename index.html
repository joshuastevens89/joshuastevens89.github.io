Joshua Stevens

Joshua Stevens had always been captivated by computers. From a young age, he could remember the blinking cursor on an old Windows computer, the green letters and flashing code that sparked his imagination. Growing up in Princeton, Texas, his fascination only deepened over time. While his classmates might have been drawn to sports or music, Joshua was the kid dismantling the family computer to see how it all worked, then putting it back together just to prove he could. His mom, who raised him and his younger brother, Izaiah, on her own, saw his natural knack for technology early on. She encouraged him, supporting his curiosity even when it meant the occasional "tech support" phone call from friends and neighbors.

Joshua's life has been a winding road of diverse experiences. He graduated from Princeton High School, known around town as the friendly, driven kid with a fascination for all things tech. After high school, he initially pursued a cosmetology degree, getting halfway through before life steered him in another direction. His hands-on skills and a natural affinity for connecting with people led him to work as a male dancer in clubs across Dallas, captivating crowds in LGBTQ spaces. This experience deepened his confidence and social savvy, allowing him to forge connections in ways he hadn’t imagined.

After his time on the dance floors, Joshua redirected his energy into the construction industry. His entrepreneurial spirit shone through as he established two successful companies: Hammertime Contracting and The Remodelers. These ventures allowed him to channel his passion for building and problem-solving, adding structure and stability to his life. The skills and resilience he’d built along the way prepared him for leadership and the challenges of running his own businesses, blending hard work with creativity. But construction wasn’t his only pursuit. Joshua’s life took yet another turn when he started working in the restaurant industry, eventually taking on a managerial role at Waffle House. His confidence, charm, and easy-going nature made him a familiar face and a cherished friend to many. The work was tough, but Joshua thrived in environments where he could be social, connect with people, and make them feel comfortable.

Through all of this, though, Joshua’s love for technology never faded. At 24, he attempted to jump into formal tech training, but life wasn’t ready to give him the green light just yet. Circumstances made it hard to commit fully to school, and he decided to wait until the timing was right. Now, in a stable environment with a steady job, Joshua has embraced the opportunity to return to his true calling. He's currently pursuing a full stack web development program at BloomTech Institute of Technology and an associate degree in software development at Dallas College, specializing in Java and Python. Learning to code and create web pages feels like coming home; he’s blending his childhood passion with new skills, mastering everything from backend logic to the design principles that make websites look sleek and polished. His innate curiosity and hands-on approach are exactly what make him excel in these fields. Joshua still holds dreams beyond his coursework. He has a few tech company ideas, complete with paperwork ready to go, but he’s taking his time, planning each move carefully to ensure that these ventures reflect his vision. He’s not just diving into the tech industry—he’s making a conscious, thoughtful entry, fortified by years of resilience, hard work, and life experience. Outside of tech, he’s passionate about health and nutrition. In his free time, you’ll often find him in the gym or researching the latest in supplementation, making sure his body has the nutrients it needs to keep up with his busy, demanding lifestyle.

Today, Joshua Stevens is a man balancing a steady job, a rigorous academic schedule, and a thriving social life. He’s transformed his childhood fascination into a real-life pursuit, equipping himself with the skills to shape his own future in technology. His journey—from the boy who loved the flashing cursor to the manager, student, and entrepreneur—is one marked by determination, adaptability, and a love for learning that has carried him through every twist and turn.

Zack Pearlman

Zack Pearlman’s journey into comedy and acting started in an unlikely place: Ann Arbor, Michigan. Born on May 19, 1988, Zack grew up in a supportive family that recognized his knack for humor early on. From making his classmates laugh in high school to honing his skills in improv and sketch comedy, Zack always had a natural ability to make people see the funny side of life.

Zack attended Pioneer High School in Ann Arbor, and though he was talented and funny, he didn’t have the typical path to Hollywood stardom. After high school, he briefly considered college, but his passion for acting and making people laugh was too strong. He threw himself into comedy, performing locally and online, knowing that the conventional route wasn’t for him. Zack’s big break came in an unconventional way: through a YouTube audition video. He entered a nationwide talent search for a role in the raunchy 2010 comedy The Virginity Hit, produced by comedy giants Will Ferrell and Adam McKay. The movie, though not a blockbuster, made a mark, and Zack’s unique comedic presence and sense of timing caught the industry’s attention. It was the stepping stone he needed, proving that his instincts to jump straight into acting were spot-on.

Zack soon found himself cast in The Inbetweeners, an American adaptation of the popular British series. Playing the role of Jay, Zack’s brand of humor—a mix of absurdity and sincerity—shone through, helping him gain a solid following. Although the show was short-lived, Zack’s performance left an impression, showcasing his adaptability and commitment to his craft. Afterward, he continued building his résumé with appearances in popular shows like Key & Peele, Workaholics, and Shameless. Each role, whether big or small, demonstrated his versatility and unique comedic flair. His next major role came in Mulaney, where he played "Big Deal." Though the series wasn’t a long-term hit, Zack’s portrayal of the quirky, lovable character was memorable, adding a layer of humor and warmth to the show. Perhaps one of his most notable achievements came with his role as Levon in Wet Hot American Summer: First Day of Camp and Wet Hot American Summer: Ten Years Later, Netflix’s revival of the cult classic series. Zack’s character blended into the chaotic world of Camp Firewood perfectly, and he managed to stand out even in a cast filled with established comedy icons. These roles further cemented Zack as a dynamic actor capable of holding his own in an ensemble cast.

Behind the scenes, Zack continued to work on his craft, branching into voice acting for animated shows and taking on diverse roles. He’s become known for his quick wit, comedic timing, and ability to bring a relatable warmth to every character he plays. Today, Zack Pearlman’s career reflects the journey of someone who pursued his dreams with resilience and humor. He’s a testament to the idea that there’s no single path to success in the entertainment industry, and his story shows how passion, determination, and a bit of humor can open doors that might otherwise remain closed. Zack Pearlman’s name is now recognized by audiences who appreciate his humor, his humility, and his ability to bring laughter to screens big and small.

From an early age, Joshua Stevens was entranced by the world of technology. Growing up in Princeton, Texas, he found himself spellbound by the flickering screen of an old Windows computer. The first time he saw that blinking cursor, it felt like magic. He was the kid who would take apart gadgets just to see how they worked, reassembling them with a newfound understanding. His bedroom was cluttered with computer parts, manuals, and wires—each component a puzzle waiting to be solved. Joshua loved building computers from scratch, troubleshooting them when they malfunctioned, and eventually mastering the art of programming. He soaked up every bit of knowledge he could find, pouring over books and online resources, teaching himself the intricacies of coding.

After high school, he initially ventured into cosmetology but soon realized that his passion lay elsewhere. At 24, Joshua decided to pursue a career in technology, enrolling in classes that would set the foundation for his journey in software development. His love for coding blossomed as he immersed himself in the world of programming languages, algorithms, and data structures. Joshua discovered a particular fascination with Java and Python, admiring their versatility and application in real-world scenarios.

To further his education, he enrolled at Dallas College, where he is pursuing an associates degree in software development. The experience is invigorating; he finds himself surrounded by like-minded individuals who share his enthusiasm for technology. The late-night study sessions, collaborative projects, and countless cups of coffee fuel his desire to learn. Each completed project brings a sense of accomplishment, from creating simple applications to developing more complex systems. Simultaneously, Joshua seeks out additional knowledge at BloomTech Institute of Technology, where he is tackling full stack web development. The combination of front-end design and back-end functionality fascinates him, and he embraces the challenge of mastering both sides. He loves crafting user-friendly interfaces while ensuring robust server-side logic, finding joy in bringing his ideas to life. The thrill of seeing a website he is coding come together, functioning seamlessly, ignites a sense of pride and fulfillment within him.

Throughout this journey, Joshua never lost sight of his childhood curiosity. He frequently experiments with personal projects, building websites and applications that showcased his growing skill set. His home office has become a hub of creativity, filled with sketches of ideas and code snippets, as he pushes the boundaries of what he can create. As he continues his studies, Joshua is also laying the groundwork for future endeavors. He has a few tech company ideas brewing in his mind, and he meticulously documented them, eager to develop the details. He envisions solutions that could simplify everyday tasks, enhance productivity, and even revolutionize industries. While he has the paperwork ready for these ventures, he knows the importance of thorough planning and execution.

Joshua’s passion for technology extends beyond coding; he is also an advocate for health and wellness in the tech space. He understands the importance of balance, making sure he takes care of his physical and mental well-being while navigating the demanding world of software development. In the gym, he often finds inspiration for his projects, considering how technology could intersect with health, nutrition, and fitness.

Today, Joshua Stevens stands at the precipice of his future in technology. Pursuing a solid education in software development and web programming, he will soon be ready to make his mark in the tech industry. His journey—rooted in a childhood fascination with computers and fueled by relentless curiosity—has equipped him with the skills to shape the digital world. Each line of code he writes is a step toward a future he’s eager to create, and the possibilities ahead are as endless as the blinking cursor that first ignited his passion so many years ago.
